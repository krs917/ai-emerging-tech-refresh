Course Overview

This course invites students to explore artificial intelligence and the technologies shaping the future of work, society, and human interaction. Building on 9th-grade digital foundations, it develops AI Fluency — working with AI effectively, efficiently, ethically, and safely. Students open the black box of machine learning, design and red-team a simple AI agent, evaluate the 2026 technology landscape with evidence rather than hype, and build an authentic professional digital identity.

The redesign consolidates twelve short units into six deeper arcs, threads Anthropic's AI Fluency framework through every one, and merges the two former culminating projects into a single seven-meeting capstone pitched to an authentic panel.

Lasallian connection: grounded in the mission of faith and discovery, students learn to treat innovation as understanding and stewardship — enhancing the human experience with integrity, wisdom, and hope. The course's emphasis on Diligence is a direct expression of this mission.

The Course Spine: AI Fluency

Four competencies for professional-grade human-AI collaboration, practiced in every arc and assessed directly in the capstone.

Delegation

What should AI do — and what should stay human?

Task analysis, goal setting, deciding whether, when, and how to engage AI.

Description

How do I communicate what I need?

Clear prompts, rich context, specified formats and constraints.

Discernment

Is this output any good?

Evaluating accuracy, quality, bias, and fit; iterating deliberately.

Diligence

Am I being responsible?

Verifying claims, disclosing AI use, protecting privacy, owning the product.

Automation

AI performs the task. Signature move: precise specification.

Augmentation

Human and AI as thinking partners. Signature move: iterative dialogue.

Agency

AI acts independently on your behalf — the defining shift of 2026. Signature move: guardrails and oversight.
Standards alignment
  • ISTE — Knowledge Constructor: students critically curate global reports (WEF, Microsoft WorkLab, MIT Technology Review) into visualizations, forecasts, and solutions.
  • OECD/EC AILit Framework (2026): arcs map to all four domains — Engage, Create, Manage, and Design with AI — the framework behind the PISA 2029 AI-literacy assessment.
  • Anthropic AI Fluency Framework: the 4Ds threaded through every arc, graded via collaboration logs and delegation charts.
